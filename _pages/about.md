My research interests include **computer vision**, **continual learning**, and **knowledge distillation**, with a focus on class-incremental visual recognition and memory-efficient lifelong learning for image classification and object detection.

Current research
======
**Knowledge Distillation for Class-Incremental Visual Recognition** (2026 – Present)

I study catastrophic forgetting in class-incremental object detection on the COCO benchmark, where a single detector must absorb new object categories sequentially while retaining prior ones—framing the problem as a measurable stability–plasticity trade-off.

- Reproducing and analyzing continual-learning baselines: Learning without Forgetting (LwF), iCaRL, Elastic Response Distillation (ERD), and YOLO-IOD
- Designing teacher–student schemes that distill logit- and feature-level knowledge from a frozen prior-task detector
- Rebuilding the YOLO-World open-vocabulary detection backbone with newer-generation YOLO architectures
- Quantifying retention and plasticity under standard incremental-detection protocols—per-step and average mAP, forgetting, and backward/forward transfer
